What Is Legal Process Outsourcing and How It Supports U.S. Law Firms

Legal Process Outsourcing (LPO) is the practice of delegating routine legal support tasks to specialized external teams, allowing attorneys to focus on high-value legal strategy and client relationships.

Unlike traditional staffing agencies, LPO providers like Ocean Clicks employ legal-trained professionals who understand U.S. case workflows and compliance requirements.

Our teams handle tasks such as petition drafting, legal research, and document review—all under attorney supervision and aligned with your firm's quality standards.

This model allows law firms to scale case capacity efficiently and reduce overhead costs during peak filing periods.

Common Legal Outsourcing Scenarios We Support

Real situations where law firms benefit from structured legal process outsourcing

When your firm experiences a surge in bankruptcy cases—whether due to seasonal patterns, economic conditions, or successful marketing campaigns—our team can handle petition preparation, schedule drafting, and filing documentation without requiring you to hire and train additional paralegal staff.
Credit reporting disputes require detailed credit report analysis and compliance-aligned documentation. Our FCRA-trained specialists can process dispute letters, analyze credit reports, and prepare litigation support documents, allowing your attorneys to focus on case strategy and client representation.
Fair Debt Collection Practices Act cases demand meticulous documentation and compliance tracking. We provide support with complaint review, evidence compilation, and case file organization while maintaining strict adherence to FDCPA requirements and documentation standards.
Complex litigation matters often require extensive legal research, case law analysis, and memoranda preparation. Our legal research team can handle research assignments, prepare case summaries, and draft preliminary legal documents under attorney supervision.
Client intake, initial case setup, and ongoing case administration can consume valuable attorney time. Our virtual paralegal assistants manage intake workflows, organize case files, track deadlines, and coordinate communications, freeing your legal team to focus on substantive legal work.
