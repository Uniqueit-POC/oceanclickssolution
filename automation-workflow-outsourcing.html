Compliance & Security in Automated Workflows

Automation must follow compliance — not override it. At OceanClicks, automation is used to improve speed, consistency, and traceability within the boundaries of U.S. consumer protection laws. Our workflows are intentionally designed to support FCRA- and FDCPA-aligned processes, with human oversight built in at every critical step.

FCRA Compliance Coverage

Our compliance-first automation framework helps protect clients from common statutory risks

§ 609

Information Disclosure & Factual Accuracy

Automated processes ensure that disputes and audits are grounded in information obtained through proper consumer disclosures, with checks to prevent factual misrepresentation or incomplete disclosure.

15 U.S.C. § 1681e(b)

Reasonable Procedures to Assure Accuracy

Workflows are structured to support reasonable procedures for accuracy, including standardized review steps, documentation checks, and exception handling where discrepancies are identified.

§ 1681g

Full File Disclosure

Automation supports proper handling and organization of full-file disclosures, ensuring that reviews and audits are based on complete consumer report data rather than partial extracts.

§ 1681i

Reinvestigation Requirements

Reinvestigation timelines, responses, and outcomes are tracked systematically to support compliance with statutory reinvestigation obligations.

§ 1681i

Consumer Statements

Processes allow for identification and tracking of consumer statements associated with disputed information, ensuring they are not overlooked during review or follow-up.

§ 1681i

Methods of Verification

Automation assists in organizing and tracking verification responses without substituting or fabricating verification steps, maintaining audit-ready records for downstream review.

§ 623

Furnisher Reporting Responsibilities

Workflows are designed to flag potential furnisher-related issues and ensure that dispute handling aligns with furnisher obligations under the FCRA.

FDCPA Compliance Considerations

Automation supports structured handling of dispute communications, documentation, and timelines to reduce exposure to:

Improper reporting practices
Inconsistent documentation trails
Missed or mishandled consumer communications

All FDCPA-sensitive activities are executed with clear process boundaries and human review.
