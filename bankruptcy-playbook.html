Why Structured Bankruptcy Preparation Matters

Bankruptcy filings demand precision, documentation control, and strict adherence to procedural timelines. Inconsistent intake processes, missing documentation, or misaligned petition preparation can delay filings and increase compliance risk. This playbook outlines a structured approach to bankruptcy case preparation and back-end workflow management.

01

Phase 1

Client Intake & Financial Data Collection

Accurate filings begin with structured intake and financial documentation control.

•Collect income documentation (pay stubs, tax returns, W-2s)
•Gather full debt schedules and creditor lists
•Verify asset disclosures
•Confirm property ownership records
•Review secured vs unsecured debt classification
•Log case in internal case management system

Every case must begin with complete and verified financial data.

02

Phase 2

Petition Drafting & Schedule Preparation

Drafting must follow standardized internal workflow to reduce errors.

Quality Control Required

•Prepare voluntary petition
•Draft Schedules A/B through J
•Verify Statement of Financial Affairs accuracy
•Cross-check creditor matrix
•Review means test calculations (if applicable)
•Conduct internal quality control review before attorney approval
03

Phase 3

Documentation Review & Compliance Checkpoints

Pre-filing review prevents delays and corrections.

•Verify all required signatures
•Confirm exemption planning review
•Validate debtor identification documents
•Confirm filing fee arrangements
•Conduct pre-filing checklist confirmation
•Ensure document formatting aligns with court requirements
04

Phase 4

Post-Filing Workflow Management

Operational structure continues after filing.

•Track court notices and deadlines
•Monitor 341 meeting scheduling
•Maintain updated case file organization
•Log trustee communications
•Track reaffirmation agreements
•Update case status in internal system
05

Phase 5

Audit Trail & Case Documentation Control

Documentation Control Framework

•Maintain structured digital case folder
•Preserve document version history
•Log attorney approvals
•Track client communication records
•Store court correspondence systematically

Structured documentation ensures audit-readiness and reduces operational risk.

Operational Insight

Bankruptcy case preparation is not administrative support — it is precision workflow management. Structured outsourcing improves consistency, reduces errors, and allows attorneys to focus on strategy and client advisory.
